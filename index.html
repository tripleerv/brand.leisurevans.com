Typography

Headings

Primary headings use Chronicle Italic 600.

Everything in moderation. Chronicle is a regal, high contrast font that exudes luxury and quality. While these are two hallmarks of Leisure Travel Vans, there can be too much of a good thing. Chronicle should only be used in moderation. Main headings. Nothing more.

Chronicle should be used in it’s italic form to accentuate motion.

Secondary headings use Gotham.

Legibility and friendly confidence. That’s what Gotham brings to the table. The general rule of thumb is to use Gotham for any subheads, pull quotes, digital headlines and small, isolated bodies of copy.

Tertiary headings use Proxima Nova.

Proxima Nova was chosen for its clarity as well as its parallels with the parent brand, Triple E Canada.

For sub-heading use, Proxima should be set in BOLD ALL CAPS.

Body

Proxima Nova will be also used anywhere that is not a heading. This could include body copy, fine print, or some sub-headings.
